The Americans with Disabilities Act is a Federal civil rights law that prohibits the exclusion of people with disabilities from everyday activities, such as enjoying a meal at a restaurant. For additional information regarding wheelchair accessibility compliance, you can refer to the ADA, the Americans with Disabilities Act website.If it is not readily achievable to provide the minimal number of accessible tables in all areas where fixed tables are provided, then services must be provided in another accessible location. NOTE: Alternative locations must be available for all customers and not just people with disabilities. It is illegal to segregate people with disabilities in one area by designating it as an accessible area to be used only by people with disabilities.
